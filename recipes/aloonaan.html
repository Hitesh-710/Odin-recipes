Aloo Naan

Description

Aloo naan is a soft, leavened Indian flatbread stuffed with a flavorful potato filling. Made with all-purpose flour, yogurt, and yeast or baking powder, the dough is kneaded to perfection and allowed to rest for a fluffy texture. The potato stuffing is spiced with ingredients like cumin, coriander, green chilies, and garam masala, creating a delicious and aromatic blend. Once assembled, the naan is rolled out and cooked in a tandoor or on a hot skillet until golden and slightly charred. Aloo naan is best enjoyed with curries, yogurt, or pickles, offering a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup warm milk (adjust as needed)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Butter or ghee for brushing
  • Nigella seeds or sesame seeds for garnish

Steps

  1. Mix flour, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, salt, yogurt, and warm milk to form a soft dough; cover and let it rest for 1-2 hours.
  2. Mash boiled potatoes and mix in cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, green chili, fresh coriander, and salt to prepare the filling.
  3. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each into a small disc.
  4. Place a spoonful of potato filling in the center of the disc, seal the edges, and gently flatten it.
  5. Roll the stuffed dough into a naan shape using a rolling pin, being careful not to tear it.
  6. Cook the naan on a hot skillet or tawa until golden spots appear on both sides, flipping as needed.
  7. Brush with butter or ghee, garnish with nigella seeds or sesame seeds if desired, and serve hot.
