Suji Ka Halva

Description

Suji ka halwa, or semolina pudding, is a classic Indian dessert made from roasted semolina, ghee, sugar, and water or milk. The semolina is toasted in ghee until golden and aromatic, then simmered with a sweetened liquid to create a soft, smooth texture. Often flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts like almonds, cashews, and raisins, suji ka halwa is rich, comforting, and mildly sweet. It is a popular dish for festive occasions, religious ceremonies, or as a quick homemade treat.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina (suji)
  • 3/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 cups water or milk (or a mix of both)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 tablespoons chopped nuts (almonds, cashews)
  • 1 tablespoon raisins (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat ghee in a pan and roast the semolina (suji) on medium heat until golden and aromatic, stirring continuously.
  2. In a separate pot, heat water or milk until warm; add sugar and stir until dissolved.
  3. Gradually pour the sweetened liquid into the roasted semolina while stirring to avoid lumps.
  4. Cook on low heat, stirring continuously, until the mixture thickens and the semolina absorbs the liquid.
  5. Add cardamom powder, mix well, and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  6. Garnish with chopped nuts and raisins, stir, and serve warm.
