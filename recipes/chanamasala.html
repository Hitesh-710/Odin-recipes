Chana Masala

Description

Chana Masala is a popular and flavorful vegetarian dish from Indian cuisine, primarily made with chickpeas (chana) cooked in a rich, spiced tomato-based gravy. The dish is characterized by a balance of tangy, savory, and mildly spicy flavors. Key ingredients include onions, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, and a variety of spices such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and chili powder. Some variations may also include ingredients like cilantro, green chilies, or a touch of lemon juice to enhance the taste. The chickpeas absorb the aromatic spices, creating a hearty and satisfying dish. Chana Masala is often served with rice, naan, or roti, making it a staple in many Indian households and restaurants worldwide. It's known for being both nutritious and filling, offering a good source of protein and fiber.

Ingredients

  • 1-2 cups (soaked overnight or canned)
  • Onion: 1 large, finely chopped
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium, chopped or pureed
  • Garlic: 3-4 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, minced or grated
  • Green chilies: 1-2, slit (optional, for heat)

Steps

  1. Soak chickpeas overnight and boil them until soft.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and saut√© chopped onions until golden.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  4. Mix in chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Add turmeric, cumin, coriander, red chili powder, and garam masala; stir well.
  6. Add boiled chickpeas along with some of their cooking water.
  7. Simmer until the gravy thickens.
  8. Add salt to taste, garnish with chopped coriander, and serve hot.
